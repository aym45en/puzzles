Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants and some other organisms use sunlight to synthesize foods with carbon dioxide and water. It produces oxygen as a byproduct.
Cellular respiration is the process in which cells convert glucose and oxygen into energy (ATP), carbon dioxide, and water.
Deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) is the molecule that carries genetic instructions used in growth, development, functioning, and reproduction of all living organisms.
An atom is the basic unit of a chemical element, consisting of a nucleus of protons and neutrons, with electrons orbiting this nucleus.
Evolution is the process through which species of organisms undergo genetic changes over time, leading to variations and the emergence of new species.
The Big Bang Theory posits that the universe began from an extremely hot and dense state about 13.8 billion years ago and has been expanding ever since.
Quantum mechanics is a fundamental theory in physics that describes nature at the smallest scales, such as particles like electrons and photons.
Einstein's theory of relativity includes the special and general theories, which describe the relationship between space, time, and gravity.
Black holes are regions of space where the gravitational pull is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from them.
Climate change refers to significant changes in global temperatures and weather patterns over time, primarily due to human activities like burning fossil fuels.
Plate tectonics is the theory explaining the movement of Earth's lithospheric plates, leading to earthquakes, volcanic activity, and continental drift.
Genetics is the study of genes, genetic variation, and heredity in living organisms.
Mitosis is the process of cell division that results in two genetically identical daughter cells from a single parent cell.
Meiosis is a type of cell division that reduces the number of chromosomes by half, producing four genetically diverse gametes.
Natural selection is the process by which organisms better adapted to their environment tend to survive and produce more offspring.
The electromagnetic spectrum includes all types of electromagnetic radiation, from gamma rays to radio waves.
Newton's laws describe the relationship between the motion of an object and the forces acting on it.
Thermodynamics is the study of heat, energy, and the work done by them, governed by the laws of thermodynamics.
An ecosystem is a community of living organisms interacting with their physical environment.
Biodiversity refers to the variety and variability of life on Earth, encompassing different species, ecosystems, and genetic variations.
Osmosis is the movement of water molecules through a selectively permeable membrane from an area of lower solute concentration to an area of higher solute concentration.
Acids are substances that release hydrogen ions (H+) in solution, while bases release hydroxide ions (OH-).
Mendelian inheritance refers to the patterns of inheritance of traits discovered by Gregor Mendel, based on dominant and recessive alleles.
Ribonucleic acid (RNA) is a molecule essential for various biological roles, including coding, decoding, regulation, and expression of genes.
Fossil fuels, such as coal, oil, and natural gas, are energy sources formed from the remains of ancient plants and animals.
A genetic mutation is a change in the DNA sequence that can lead to variations in traits and sometimes diseases.
Symbiosis is a close and often long-term interaction between two different biological species.
Antibiotics are substances used to kill or inhibit the growth of bacteria, helping to treat bacterial infections.
Vaccination is the administration of a vaccine to stimulate an individual's immune system to develop adaptive immunity to a pathogen.
Heredity is the passing of traits from parents to offspring through genes.
CRISPR-Cas9 is a genetic engineering tool that allows for precise modifications to DNA in living organisms.
Chromosomes are long strands of DNA wrapped around proteins, found in the nucleus of most living cells, carrying genetic information.
Bacteria are single-celled microorganisms that can be found in various environments, some of which cause diseases.
Viruses are small infectious agents that can only replicate inside the living cells of an organism.
Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions in living organisms.
The light reactions of photosynthesis occur in the thylakoid membranes and convert solar energy into chemical energy in the form of ATP and NADPH.
The Calvin cycle, occurring in the stroma of chloroplasts, uses ATP and NADPH to convert COâ‚‚ into glucose.
Adenosine triphosphate (ATP) is the primary energy carrier in cells.
DNA replication is the process by which a DNA molecule makes a copy of itself.
Transcription is the process in which a segment of DNA is copied into RNA by the enzyme RNA polymerase.
Protein synthesis involves transcription and translation, where RNA is used to create proteins.
Mitochondria are the powerhouses of the cell, generating most of the cell's supply of ATP through cellular respiration.
Chloroplasts are organelles found in plant cells and some protists that conduct photosynthesis.
Gene expression is the process by which the instructions in a gene are used to synthesize a functional gene product, usually a protein.
Homeostasis is the ability of an organism to maintain a stable internal environment despite changes in external conditions.
Eukaryotic cells are complex cells with a nucleus and membrane-bound organelles, found in animals, plants, fungi, and protists.
Prokaryotic cells are simpler cells without a nucleus, including bacteria and archaea.
Photosynthetic pigments, such as chlorophyll, absorb light energy used in photosynthesis.
The endosymbiotic theory suggests that eukaryotic cells evolved from a symbiotic relationship between different species of prokaryotes.
Genetic drift is a mechanism of evolution that involves random changes in the frequency of alleles in a population.
Gene flow is the transfer of genetic variation from one population to another.
Speciation is the process by which new distinct species arise.
Ecosystem services are the benefits humans receive from ecosystems, including clean water, air, and pollination.
Biogeochemical cycles describe the movements of elements like carbon, nitrogen, and phosphorus between living and non-living parts of ecosystems.
The greenhouse effect is the warming of Earth's surface due to the trapping of heat by greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.
Acid rain is precipitation with a lower pH than normal, caused by the release of sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides into the atmosphere.
The ozone layer is a region of Earth's stratosphere that absorbs most of the Sun's ultraviolet radiation.
Biodiversity hotspots are regions with a high level of endemic species that are under threat from human activities.
Extinction is the end of an organism or a group of organisms, typically a species.
Genetic engineering involves the direct manipulation of an organism's genome using biotechnology.
Stem cells are undifferentiated cells with the potential to develop into different cell types.
Bioluminescence is the production and emission of light by living organisms.
The efficiency of photosynthesis is determined by how effectively plants convert light energy into chemical energy.
Epigenetics is the study of heritable changes in gene expression that do not involve changes to the underlying DNA sequence.
The microbiome is the collection of microorganisms living in and on the human body, playing crucial roles in health and disease.
